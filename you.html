Thank you for reaching out.


Getting help for disordered eating can be scary. Find help and resources below.

Below, you'll find a few steps to take to start the process of receiving help. It is always a good idea to go to your doctor or nutritionist.


One.


Identify adults or support figures in your life who you trust.


If you're a minor, you may need their permission to seek treatment, depending on your location. If you can't trust your guardians, it's ok to turn to a relative, neighbor, teacher, or school counselor. They can help you identify what the best next steps would be to help you receive treatment.

It is common to be struggling with an eating disorder and not know about it. Additionally, eating disorders are complex, and symptoms vary between conditions. However, if you're experiencing any of the following:

  • You are overwhelmed by thoughts about weight, size, or diet.
  • You practice behaviors such as purging, fasting, taking laxatives, or exercising to control your weight, or you binge large amount of food.
  • You struggle with body image and are uncomfortable with the way you look.


    • Two.


    Identify warning signs that could suggest it's time to seek help.


    Take a look at treatment centers near you, or ask you doctor about some.
