Helping others.

If someone you love is struggling with an eating disorder, there are things you can do to help.






Remember to preserve your own mental health while offering assistance to someone else.



Stay away from offering medical advice. Focus on emotional support and reaching out.


One.


Ask and listen.


Allowing your friend or family member to be heard out and able to share their stories may help them process their own emotions. Try to assess their situation and identify a few things that may be triggering them.

If the situation is severe enough, contact your friend or family member's closer relatives, doctor, or teacher. Letting people who spend more time with them know can help them receive round-the-clock support and assistance.


Two.


Get in touch with the patient's support.


Think of yourself, too.

Helping someone through trauma can be mentally draining. Refer yourself to a counselor or mental health professional if you find yourself feeling overwhelmed or drained.
